My profession is to research alpine natural hazards from glacial and periglacial environments. Following my personal vision, I have been working on different topics and mass movements, including snow avalanches, river floods, permafrost creep, and glacier lake outburst floods. I am always been fascinated by nature, and being an engineer, I have the tendency to think in an analythical fashion about the physical processes that shape our environment.
Currently, I am working on two different but interconnected aspects of alpine natural hazards: understanding the physical processes that control their dynamics, and managing the risk that they pose to humans.


Since 2021, I am a researcher in EClim Group within the Glaciology and Geomorphodynamics unit (3G) at the Department of Geography at the University of Zurich. During the previous two years, I have been working in the SLAB at EPFL Lausanne and at the Geomorphology Research Group of the University of Fribourg.

The website is based on a template by Daniel Viviroli and his website published on github.
Previously I shaped my academic skills at the University of Trento, at the Snow and Avalanche Research Centre SLF, and at the University of Zurich.
