--- layout: default title: About me and my research ---
My profession is to research alpine natural hazards originating from glacial and periglacial environments. Following my personal vision, I have been working over the past decade on different topics and mass movements, including snow avalanches, permafrost creep, and glacier lake outburst floods. I have always been fascinated by natureand being an engineer, I have the tendency to think in an analythical fashion about the physical processes that shape our environment.
Currently, I am working on two different but interconnected aspects of alpine natural hazards: understanding the physical processes that control their dynamics, and managing the risk that they pose to humans.


Since 2021, I am a researcher in the EClim Group within the Glaciology and Geomorphodynamics unit (3G) at the Department of Geography of the University of Zurich. During the previous two years, I have been working in the SLAB Laboratory at EPFL Lausanne and at the Geomorphology Research Group at the University of Fribourg.
